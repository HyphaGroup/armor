# Digital Security Clinic

## **Overview**

The **Digital Security Clinic** provides a structured, high-level, guided approach to enhancing digital security. By assessing participants' current practices, prioritizing risks, and implementing actionable remediation strategies, the clinic effectively empowers them to adopt essential digital security best practices. The remote, interactive format ensures accessibility and fosters a collaborative environment where we can learn from each other and build a resilient digital security posture.

### **Objectives**

- **Assess** your and your team’s current digital security posture.
- **Identify** key areas for improvement based on best practices.
- **Develop** actionable remediation strategies.
- **Foster** ongoing digital security awareness and resilience.

### **Format**

- **Remote Engagement:** Utilizing video conferencing, collaborative documents, and secure communication channels.
- **Individual or Small Groups:** Personalized sessions for individuals or teams of up to 5 members.
- **Interactive Sessions:** Combining screen-sharing, discussions, and hands-on activities.
- **Resource Materials:** Checklists, Action Plans, and other structured documents outlining remediation steps and best practice improvements.
- **Follow-Up Resources:** Access to additional guides, tutorials, and support channels.

## **Clinic Workflow and Structure**

### Stage 0: Introductions and Session Flow

1. **Welcome and Introductions**

  - **Facilitator Introduction:** Share background, expertise, and role in the clinic.
  - **Participant Introductions:** Participants share their name and role, as well as a brief overview of why they are here.
2. **Overview of the Clinic**

  - **Objectives:** Recap the clinic's goals and what participants can expect to achieve.
  - **Agenda:** Present the session flow, including time allocations for each stage.
  - **Ground Rules:** Establish guidelines for participation, confidentiality, and respectful communication.
3. **Technical Setup**

  - Ensure all participants can access necessary tools (e.g., Zoom, Google Docs, Signal).
  - Provide a quick tutorial or troubleshooting tips for using the platforms.

#### **Outputs**

- **Aligned Expectations:** Participants understand the clinic’s purpose and what they aim to achieve.
- **Engaged Participants:** A collaborative and open environment is established for effective learning and interaction.

### Stage 2: Prioritizing Improvements

#### **Purpose**

Help participants prioritize identified security gaps based on their potential impact and the organization's capacity to address them.

#### **Activities**

1. **Review Assessment Findings**

  - Present a summary of the Guided Self-Assessment results.
  - Highlight common security gaps and unique challenges the participant is facing.
2. **Risk Prioritization Workshop**

  - **Introduction to Risk Matrix:**

    - Explain the concept of likelihood vs. impact.
    - Demonstrate how to categorize risks using a simple matrix.
  - **Interactive Activity:**

    - Discuss and rank the identified risks.
    - Collaborative tools like Google Sheets or Miro can be used to map out the risks in the matrix.
    - Review the prioritized list and agree on top priorities
3. **Developing a Prioritized Risk List**

  - Compile a focused list of high-priority security improvements.
  - Ensure each priority is actionable and aligns with the resources and capabilities.

#### **Outputs**

- **Prioritized Risk List:** A clear and focused list of security issues to address first.
- **Actionable Insights:** Understanding which areas to address first for maximum security enhancement.

### Stage 3: Developing Remediation Strategies

#### **Purpose**

Create actionable plans to address the prioritized security risks identified earlier

#### **Activities**

1. **Best Practices Overview**

  - Present high-level strategies for key security areas such as:

    - **Authentication and Access Control**

      - **Assessment:** Evaluate the current use of two-factor authentication (2FA) and password management.
      - **Remediation:** Develop strategies to implement 2FA across all accounts and encourage the use of password managers.
    - **Secure Communication Tools**

      - **Assessment:** Identify current communication tools (email, messaging, video conferencing) and their security configurations.
      - **Remediation:** Recommend secure alternatives (e.g., Signal for messaging, Zoom with enhanced security settings) and guide on transitioning.
    - **Data Management and Backups**

      - **Assessment:** Review current data storage practices, retention priorities, and backup procedures.
      - **Remediation:** Implement regular data backups, deletion, and using secure services (e.g., Google Cloud) and ensure data encryption.
    - **Device Security**

      - **Assessment:** Examine current practices for securing desktops, laptops, and mobile devices.
      - **Remediation:** Enforce regular software updates, full disk encryption, and secure Wi-Fi configurations.
    - **Social Media Security**

      - **Assessment:** Analyze current social media usage and personal information exposure.
      - **Remediation:** Provide guidelines for securing social media accounts, managing personal information, and using privacy tools.
2. **Remediation Strategy Development**

  - **Collaborative Planning:**

    - Guide participants in outlining specific steps to implement best practices.
    - Use templates to structure the remediation plans.
  - **Resource Allocation:**

    - Identify necessary resources (tools, training, budget) required to implement each remediation step.
  - **Timeline Creation:**

    - Develop a realistic timeline for implementing the remediation strategies.
3. **Action Plan Documentation**

  - Document the remediation strategies in a structured format.
  - Ensure each action item is clear, assignable, and time-bound.

#### **Outputs**

- **Action Plans:** Detailed steps each organization will take to improve their digital security.
- **Implementation Timeline:** Schedule outlining when and how each remediation action will be executed.

### Stage 4: Hands-on Implementation

#### **Purpose**

Provide practical support to help participants begin implementing their remediation strategies.

#### **Activities**

1. **Interactive Workshops**

  - **Focus Areas:**

    - Authentication setup (e.g., enabling 2FA)
    - Configuring secure communication tools (e.g., setting up Signal)
    - Implementing data backup solutions
    - Enhancing device security (e.g., enabling encryption)
  - **Guided Exercises:**

    - Step-by-step walkthroughs for setting up key security measures.
    - Real-time assistance and troubleshooting.
2. **Q&A Sessions**

  - Open the floor for participants to ask questions related to their specific implementation challenges.
  - Provide expert advice and solutions to common problems.
3. **Facilitator Support**

  - Offer one-on-one assistance for complex issues.
  - Share best practices and practical tips for effective implementation.

#### **Outputs**

- **Progress Milestones:** Achieved initial steps towards implementing action plans.
- **Enhanced Understanding:** Improved comprehension of how to apply best practices effectively.

### Stage 5: Ongoing Network Support & Monitoring

#### **Purpose**

Ensure sustained improvement and adaptation of digital security practices over time.

#### **Activities**

1. **Establish Support Channels**

  - **Dedicated Communication Groups:** Create a Signal group or similar secure channel for ongoing assistance.
  - **Contact Points:** Provide contact information for continued expert support and troubleshooting.
2. **Follow-Up Sessions**

  - **Regular Check-Ins:** Schedule periodic meetings (e.g., monthly or quarterly) to review progress.
  - **Progress Reviews:** Discuss what has been implemented, successes, and any new challenges.
  - **Adjust Action Plans:** Update remediation strategies based on progress and evolving needs.
3. **Continuous Improvement**

  - **Regular Assessments:** Encourage participants to perform periodic security assessments.
  - **Stay Updated:** Share updates on new digital security best practices and emerging threats.
  - **Promote Education:** Offer resources and training opportunities to keep staff informed and vigilant.
4. **Network Building**

  - **Create a Support Network:** Encourage participants to join a community for ongoing support and knowledge sharing.
  - **Share Success Stories:** Highlight organizations that have successfully improved digital security through the clinic.

#### **Outputs**

- **Sustained Security Enhancements:** Continuous improvement in digital security posture.
- **Community Building:** Foster a network of nonprofits supporting each other in maintaining robust digital security.
